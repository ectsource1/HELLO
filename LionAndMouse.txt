About the story
This lesson uses fables to explore the idea of kind deeds and helping others in turn, using fables. 
Reinforces that a kind deed is never wasted and demonstrates that kindness is related to good citizenship. 
It increases listening comprehension and the use of critical thinking skills.

PAGE_1:page1.jpg
Once when a lion, the king of the jungle, was asleep, 
a little mouse began running up and down on him

PAGE_2:page8.jpg
This soon awakened the lion, who placed his huge paw on the mouse, 
and opened his big jaws to swallow him.

PAGE_3:page2.jpg
"Pardon, O King!" cried the little Mouse, "Forgive me this time. 
I shall never repeat it and I shall never forget your kindness. 
And who knows, I may be able to do you a good turn one of these days!”

PAGE_4:page3.jpg
The Lion was so tickled by the idea of the mouse being able to help him that he lifted his paw and let him go.

PAGE_5:page4.jpg
Sometime later, a few hunters captured the lion, and tied him to a tree. 
After that they went in search of a wagon, to take him to the zoo.

PAGE_6:page5.jpg
Just then the little mouse happened to pass by. On seeing the lion’s plight, 
he ran up to him and gnawed away the ropes that bound him, the king of the jungle.

PAGE_7:page6.jpg
"Was I not right?" said the little mouse, very happy to help the lion.

PAGE_8:page7.jpg
MORAL: Small acts of kindness will be rewarded greatly.
